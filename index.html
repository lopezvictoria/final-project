    Kimpira (Stir-fried Carrot & Burdock Root)

    • Servings: 4
    • Tools: Knife, measuring cups and spoons
    • Ingredients
    • 4 cups burdock roots cut into 2 inch pieces.
    • 1/2 cup carrot cut into 2 inch pieces
    • 1 1/2 tablespoons Asian style sesame oil
    • 2 tablespoons sugar
    • 1/8 teaspoon seven-spice mixture or cayenne pepper
    • 1/4 cup soy sauce
      • 0
    • Toasted white sesame seeds for garnish
      • Directions: Cut the carrot and burdock roots into matchstick size pieces. Soak them in a large non-reactive bowl of cold water for about 20 minutes. Drain. Remove the excess water by wrapping the carrot and burdock roots into a clean dish towel. Press out as much water as possible. Heat the oil in a large skillet, medium-high heat, and then stir-fry the burdock roots and carrot for 5 to 8 minutes. Add the sugar, shichimi-togarashi or cayenne and mix well. Then add the soy sauce and stir-fry until the liquid is absorbed. Sprinkle with sesame seeds before serving. Can be served warm or at room temp.
